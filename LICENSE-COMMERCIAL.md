Frequently asked questions
--------------------------

**Why AGPL and not LGPL/MIT/Apache?**
The AGPL closes the "SaaS loophole" — it forces operators of
network-accessible derivative works to share their modifications. This
is the standard pick for dual-license commercial open source (used by
Grafana, MongoDB historically, Plausible, Mastodon, etc.) because it
gives commercial users a real reason to choose the paid track.

**I just want to read or learn from the code, do I need a commercial
license?**
No. Reading, evaluating, running locally, and modifying the code for
your own use are all fine under AGPL-3.0. You only owe AGPL
obligations when you *distribute* a derivative work or *operate* it as
a network service for others.

all rights granted by AGPL-3.0-or-later in perpetuity for the versions
already published under it.
